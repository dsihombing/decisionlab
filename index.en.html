MCDM Methods

Start with a method that matches your familiarity and the complexity of your decision problem. Each method will be documented with overview, steps, numerical examples, and implementation notes.

Recommended start

Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP)

Hierarchical, pairwise comparison-based method to derive criteria weights and rank alternatives with consistency checks.

  • Suitable for strategic decisions with qualitative and quantitative criteria.
  • Uses pairwise comparisons and consistency ratio.
  • Commonly used in project selection, vendor evaluation, and prioritization.

Learn AHP →

Ranking focus

TOPSIS

Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution, ranking alternatives based on distance to ideal and anti-ideal solutions.

  • Effective for ranking alternatives with measurable performance scores.
  • Uses normalization, weighting, and distance to ideal solutions.
  • Popular in supplier selection, location selection, and performance evaluation.

Learn TOPSIS →

Beginner friendly

Simple Additive Weighting (SAW)

Weighted sum method that evaluates and ranks alternatives using normalized scores and predefined weights.

  • Good entry point for those new to MCDM.
  • Conceptually simple and easy to implement in Excel.
  • Useful for many everyday multi-criteria decisions.

About Decision Lab

Decision Lab is created and maintained by Denny JC Sihombing, lecturer in the Information Systems Study Program at Atma Jaya Catholic University of Indonesia.

The lab serves as a focused MCDM Learning Hub, connecting theory and practice through structured explanations, numerical examples, and implementable tools that can be used by students and practitioners in real decision-making contexts.
