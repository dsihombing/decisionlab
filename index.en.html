MCDM Methods

Start with a method that matches your familiarity and the complexity of your decision problem. Each method will be documented with overviews, step-by-step procedures, numerical examples, and implementation notes.

Recommended Start

Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP)

Hierarchical, pairwise comparison-based method to derive criteria weights and rank alternatives with consistency checks.

  • Suitable for strategic decisions with qualitative and quantitative criteria
  • Uses pairwise comparisons and consistency ratio validation
  • Commonly used in project selection, vendor evaluation, and prioritization
Learn AHP →
Ranking Focus

TOPSIS

Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution, ranking alternatives based on distance to ideal and anti-ideal solutions.

  • Effective for ranking alternatives with measurable performance scores
  • Uses normalization, weighting, and distance to ideal solutions
  • Popular in supplier selection, location choice, and performance evaluation
Learn TOPSIS →
Beginner Friendly

Simple Additive Weighting (SAW)

Weighted sum method that evaluates and ranks alternatives using normalized scores and predefined weights.

  • Good entry point for those new to MCDM
  • Conceptually simple and easy to implement in spreadsheets
  • Useful for everyday multi-criteria decisions
