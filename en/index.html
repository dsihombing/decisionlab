Master Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM)

Structured guides, methods, and resources to support better decision-making in business and information systems.

Decision Lab provides structured learning materials for multi-criteria decision making, from foundational concepts to practical applications using methods such as AHP, TOPSIS, and SAW.

This laboratory is designed for students, researchers, and practitioners who want to apply decision support and analytical techniques to real-world decision problems.

MCDM Methods

Start with the method that best matches your familiarity and the complexity of your decision problem. Each method will be documented with an overview, step-by-step procedure, numerical example, and implementation notes.

Recommended starting point

Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP)

A hierarchical, pairwise comparison-based method for deriving criteria weights and ranking alternatives, with consistency checking.

  • Suitable for strategic decisions with qualitative and quantitative criteria.
  • Uses pairwise comparisons and consistency ratios.
  • Widely used in project selection, vendor evaluation, and priority setting.
Learn AHP →
Ranking-focused

TOPSIS

Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution, ranking alternatives based on their distance to ideal and anti-ideal solutions.

  • Effective for ranking alternatives with measurable performance scores.
  • Uses normalization, weighting, and distance to ideal solutions.
  • Popular for supplier selection, location selection, and performance evaluation.
Learn TOPSIS →
Beginner-friendly

Simple Additive Weighting (SAW)

A weighted sum method that evaluates and ranks alternatives using normalized scores and predefined weights.

  • A good entry point for those new to MCDM.
  • Conceptually simple and easy to implement in Excel or spreadsheets.
  • Useful for everyday multi-criteria decisions.
Learn SAW →

Learning Resources

Learning materials are designed to provide a solid conceptual understanding of each MCDM method, supported by follow-along numerical examples and practical implementation guidance.

The primary focus of Decision Lab is on conceptual clarity and practical application, with examples that are relevant to business and information systems contexts.

Learning formats
  • Step-by-step guides with conceptual explanations.
  • Fully worked numerical examples.
  • Implementation templates and worksheets.
  • Case studies from real-world applications.
  • References to key academic literature.
Alignment with teaching
  • Supports Decision Support Systems and MCDM-related course topics.
  • Can be used as supplementary material for student assignments and projects.
  • Integrated with the broader learning ecosystem on the main site.
